# Types of ODE
linear: only linear y terms  
homogeneous: $y' + p(x)y = 0$ no term without y  
autonomous: no x in equation  



# Existence and uniqueness: 
If $f \in C(D)$ and $(x_0, y_0) \in D$ then at least ONE solution.  
If $\frac{\delta f(y)}{\delta y} \in C(D)$ $\implies$ unique solution.

# Linear 1â€™st order ODE
form: $y'(x) + p(x)y(x) = f(x)$  
$f(x) = 0$ $\implies$ homogeneous ODE $\implies$ solve by speration of variables  
$f(x) \neq 0$ $\implies$ inhomogeneous 
general solution: $e^{-P(x)} \cdot (\int e^{P(x)} \cdot f(x) dx + C)$

get general solution using superposition:  
$y = y_{homo} + y_{p}$  
ansatz: $y_p(x) = c(x) \cdot y_{homo}$
Insert ansatz into the inhomogeneous ODE $\implies$  
$c(x) = \int e^{P(x)} \cdot f(x) dx +C$

# Bernoulli DE(non linear)
form: $y'(x) +p(x)y(x) = f(x) \cdot y^{\alpha}$
ansatz for $\alpha \neq {1;0}$: $u(x) = y^{1- \alpha}(x) \implies $solve this homogeneous ODE
